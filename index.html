Prompting Fundamentals Six techniques for getting better results from any conversation

Core Technique · 01

Be Clear & Specific

Front-load context, state the task upfront, and break large requests into concrete steps. The single highest-leverage prompting habit.

Coming Soon→
Core Technique · 02

Use Examples

Show Claude a sample of the format, tone, or style you want. A single reference output is worth a paragraph of abstract instructions.

Coming Soon→
Core Technique · 03

Encourage Step-by-Step Thinking

Ask Claude to "think through this step-by-step." Surfaces better answers on complex, multi-variable problems.

Coming Soon→
Core Technique · 04

Iterative Refinement

Specify exactly what to adjust — tone, length, a specific paragraph — rather than asking Claude to generically "make it better."

Coming Soon→
Core Technique · 05

Leverage Claude's Knowledge

Provide context on how you'll use the answer. Claude's responses become far more targeted when it understands the downstream purpose.

Coming Soon→
Core Technique · 06

Role-Playing & Personas

Assign expert roles — CFO, negotiation coach, senior marketer — to shift the depth and intelligence of responses dramatically.

Troubleshooting & Quality When outputs are wrong, incomplete, or not quite right

Quality

Minimizing Hallucinations

Tell Claude to say "I don't know" when uncertain. Include all relevant context in every new conversation — there is no memory between sessions.

Coming Soon→
Strategy

Complex Task Decomposition

When outputs miss steps or feel rushed, break the problem into a sequence of focused messages rather than one giant, overloaded prompt.
