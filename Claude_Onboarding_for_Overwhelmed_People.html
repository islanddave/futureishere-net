What is Claude?
The honest 60-second explanation
+

Claude is an AI assistant made by Anthropic. You talk to it by typing (or pasting text), and it responds. Think of it as a calm, endlessly patient colleague who lives in your browser.

Why it's different from Googling something
💬
It has a conversation with youNot a list of links — actual back-and-forth. Ask it to explain differently. Ask it again. It never gets frustrated.
✍️
It creates things for youLesson plans, emails, feedback, schedules, summaries — it writes them, you edit (or just use them).
🧠
It thinks with youStuck? Overwhelmed? Tell Claude what's going on and ask what to do first. It's great at untangling messy situations.
🚫
It never judges youMessy grammar, half-finished thoughts, repeating yourself — it doesn't care. It just helps.
You access Claude at claude.ai — just sign in and start typing. That's it.
🏠
What is Cowork?
Claude's extra set of hands for your computer
+

Claude (the chat) is your thinking partner. Cowork is a desktop app that lets Claude actually do things on your computer — not just talk about them.

What Cowork can do for you
📁
Work with files & foldersGot a messy downloads folder? Cowork can help you sort, rename, and organize files automatically.
📄
Read & rewrite your documentsPaste a lesson plan in, ask Claude to improve it, get it back in seconds.
🔗
Connect to Google Drive, Gmail & CalendarClaude can read your schedule, help draft emails, and find documents — all in one place.
🔁
Automate repetitive tasksThings you do the same way every week? Cowork can help you set those up once and run them automatically.
Simple version: Claude (chat) = your brain buddy. Cowork = your digital assistant who handles the boring stuff.

Lesson Planning — Fast
Never stare at a blank page again
+

Describe your student, the level, and the topic. Claude does the rest. You don't need perfect language — just tell it what you need like you'd tell a colleague.

Copy-paste these prompts ↓
📋 Full lesson plan
"Create a 45-minute ESL lesson for B1 adult learners on the topic of making phone calls at work. Include a warm-up, vocabulary list, a speaking activity, and a short homework task."
💡 When you're stuck for ideas
"I'm teaching intermediate adult learners who struggle with small talk. Give me 5 fun activity ideas I can run on Zoom. Keep them simple and low-prep."
📅 Weekly plan — multiple levels
"I'm teaching beginner, intermediate, and advanced students this week. Help me plan a different focus for each day Monday to Friday. Keep each day's plan to 3 bullet points max."
✍️
Student Feedback & Grading
The task that swallows your afternoons
+

Paste a student's writing into Claude. Ask for feedback. Done. You can review and adjust it — but the hard part is handled.

📝 Quick written feedback
"Here is a paragraph written by my B2 student. Please give 3 positive comments and 3 gentle suggestions for improvement, written in encouraging language suitable for an adult learner: [paste text here]"
🗂️ Reusable feedback template
"Create a reusable written feedback template I can use for student essays. Include sections for grammar, vocabulary, fluency, and end with a personal encouragement note."
🎙️ Speaking class notes
"I'm going to paste my rough notes from a speaking class. Please turn them into a short, professional student progress note I can send to the learner. Notes: [paste notes]"

Emails & Admin
Write less, communicate better
+

Writing emails when your brain is scattered is exhausting. Give Claude the basic idea — it'll write the actual email. You just change the name and send.

👩‍👧 Parent update email

🔄 Rescheduling (no guilt!)
"Write a short, friendly message to a student rescheduling tomorrow's lesson to Thursday at the same time. Be warm but brief — 3 sentences max."

"Write a friendly invoice reminder for a student who hasn't paid for last month's lessons. Keep the tone warm and non-confrontational. Just a nudge, not a demand."

The Brain Dump Method
For when everything is loud in your head
+

ADHD often means having everything in your head at once, then none of it when you need it. Brain dumping into Claude is one of the most useful things you can do.

1
Open Claude. Type everything. Messy is perfect.Don't organize it. Don't worry about spelling. Just pour it out — tasks, worries, half-ideas, all of it.
2
Ask Claude to sort it"Turn this into a prioritized to-do list. Put the most urgent things at the top. Label anything that can wait until next week."
3
Ask for just today's list"From that list, what are the 3 most important things I should do today only? Nothing else."
🌅 Perfect for Monday mornings
"Here's everything on my plate this week: [dump your list here]. Can you help me figure out what to tackle first and build a simple plan for each day? I have ADHD and get overwhelmed easily — please keep each day's list to 3 things max."
⏰
Time Blindness Help
Making invisible time visible
+

Time blindness is real — and exhausting. Claude can help you build realistic schedules that include buffer time, transitions, and breaks.

⏱️ Realistic daily schedule
"I need to prepare 2 lesson plans, send 4 student emails, and grade 6 written assignments today. How long will each actually take? Build me a realistic schedule with a lunch break and a mid-afternoon rest. I have ADHD so please include transition time between tasks."
🌅 Morning routine builder
"Help me build a realistic morning routine from waking up at 8am to being ready to teach at 9:30am. I have ADHD, I'm not naturally a morning person, and I tend to get distracted. Keep the steps short and achievable."
You can tell Claude you have ADHD — it will adjust its suggestions to be more realistic and ADHD-friendly. No explanation needed, no judgment.
🌀
Task Paralysis & Overwhelm
When you can't start anything
+

Some days starting anything feels impossible. Claude is exceptionally good at helping you break things into tiny steps — small enough that the first one is obvious.

🧱 Break it into tiny steps
"I need to [describe task] but I can't seem to start. I have ADHD and I'm feeling overwhelmed. Can you break this down into the smallest possible steps — like, step 1 should take less than 2 minutes?"
🤝 Body double mode
"I need to get [task] done in the next 30 minutes. Can you stay with me through it? Tell me what to do first, then check in with me after I do it."
😮‍💨 The "I'm scattered" reset
"I'm scattered, stressed, and don't know what to do first. My brain feels full. Here's everything I'm juggling: [list things]. Please help me slow down and identify ONE thing to do right now."
